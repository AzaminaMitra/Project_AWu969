The History of Yu-Gi-Oh!

Home Gallery History & rules

Origins

Yu-Gi-Oh! began as a manga series by Kazuki Takahashi in 1996. It quickly gained popularity thanks to its blend of action, mystery, and a focus on card-based duels.

Anime Series

The first anime aired in 1998, followed by the iconic Yu-Gi-Oh! Duel Monsters series in 2000, which introduced us to characters like Yugi Muto, Seto Kaiba, and the Shadow Games.

Card Game Evolution

Konami launched the trading card game in 1999. Over time, it became one of the highest-selling card games ever, with mechanics evolving through Synchro, XYZ, Pendulum, and Link Summoning.

Basic Rules (Master Rule 5)

Goal

Reduce your opponent’s Life Points from 8000 to 0 using monsters, spells, and traps.

Deck

You need a Main Deck (40–60 cards). Optional: Extra Deck (for special monsters) and a Side Deck.

turn phases

Each turn has 6 phases:
Draw Phase: Draw 1 card.
Standby Phase: Some card effects activate here.
Main Phase 1: Summon/set monsters, use spells/traps.
Battle Phase: Attack your opponent!
Main Phase 2: Set more cards (optional).
End Phase: Finish your turn.

card types

Monsters: Used to battle and defend.
Spells: One-time or ongoing magical effects.
Traps: Hidden cards that activate in response to something.

Summoning

You can Normal Summon 1 monster per turn. You can also Special Summon with card effects (like Fusion or Synchro).

Battle

Attack Position monsters deal damage.
If your monster's ATK > opponent’s ATK/DEF, you win the fight.
You take damage if your monster is weaker.
